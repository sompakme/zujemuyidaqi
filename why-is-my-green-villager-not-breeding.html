Why is my green villager not breeding?

1. What is the most common cause of not breeding?

The most common cause of not breeding is the villagers not being fed. Make sure to provide them with enough food.

2. What should I feed my green villagers to encourage breeding?

To encourage breeding, feed your green villagers with food items like bread, potatoes, carrots, or beets.

3. How many food items should I give to each villager?

For breeding, each villager should be given 12 potatoes, carrots, or beets, or 3 pieces of bread.

4. How should I give food to my green villagers?

Simply toss the food at the villagers’ feet. They will pick it up if they have empty food slots.

5. Do my green villagers need empty food slots to be able to take food?

Yes, your green villagers need to have empty food slots in order to take the food you provide them.

6. Do I need to have empty beds around my green villagers for breeding?

Yes, there should be enough unoccupied beds near your green villagers for them to breed.

7. How many beds should I have near my green villagers?

Make sure you have at least three beds near your green villagers to encourage breeding.

8. Do I need to trade with my green villagers to make them breed?

While trading with villagers is not mandatory for breeding, it may help if the villagers are not in the mood to breed.

9. Do my green villagers need a job to breed?

No, job sites are not required for villagers to breed. The breeding depends on the number of valid beds.

10. How do I make my green villagers willing to breed?

To make your green villagers willing to breed, place two villagers in close proximity, provide enough beds, and give food to each parent.

11. How do I breed green villagers in Minecraft?

To breed green villagers in Minecraft, make sure they are alone together, give them enough food (12 Beetroots, 12 Carrots, 12 Potatoes, or 3 Bread), and provide beds for them.

12. Can’t my green villagers breed because they don’t have enough beds?

Yes, if there are not enough unoccupied beds near your green villagers, they may not be able to breed.

13. How do I make my green villagers grow up?

Villagers will grow up to adults after exactly 20 minutes of being within render distance. During this time, they need to have enough beds and food.

14. Are nitwit villagers useful for breeding?

No, nitwit villagers cannot be used for breeding. They have a different routine compared to regular villagers and do not work or take up any jobs.

15. Can villagers breed in peaceful mode?

Yes, villagers can breed in peaceful mode just like in any other mode.

16. What are the chances of breeding a nitwit?

In Bedrock Edition, every baby villager has a 10% chance to become a nitwit.

17. Can I turn a nitwit into a normal villager?

No, once a villager becomes a nitwit, it cannot be turned into a normal villager. Nitwits have a different routine and cannot take up any jobs.

18. Can I speed up villager breeding?

Yes, you can speed up villager breeding by increasing the number of breedable villagers within the farm and ensuring they have enough beds and food.

19. How long does it take for a baby villager to grow up?

After exactly 20 minutes of being within render distance, a baby villager will grow up to an adult.

20. Will villager breeding work in peaceful mode?

Yes, villager breeding will work in peaceful mode just like in any other mode.

21. What is a nitwit villager?

A nitwit villager is a special type of villager that has a different routine compared to regular villagers. Nitwits do not work and cannot take up any jobs.

22. Can nitwits be used in iron farms?

No, nitwits cannot be used in iron farms as they do not summon iron golems.
